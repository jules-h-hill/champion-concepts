Core fantasy

A bruiser who creates forced decisions instead of stat-checking you to death.

Sevaran arrives when the scales tip. His kit is built on a single threshold — 50% health — and bends every fight toward it. Punish the strong, preserve the weak, and make both teams play by his terms.

Half-order, half-chaos armor. A chained judgment halo. A dual gavel-warhammer, and scales worked into every plate. He is not dashing into the fight. He is intervening.

Balance Must Be Kept

Passive

Sevaran gains bonus damage or defensive value when fighting a target whose health state is meaningfully different from his own — sharpest around the 50% line. The wider the imbalance, the harder he tips it back.

Identity tie: his entire kit is one idea expressed five ways — punish the high, preserve the low.

Sentence

Q · basic

A short cone hammer swing deals physical damage and marks enemies hit with Sentence. Sevaran's next basic attack consumes the mark for bonus damage and a brief slow. The bonus is higher against targets above 50% HP — and if Sevaran is himself below 50%, consuming the mark heals him.

Why it works: a normal bruiser trade tool that rewards weaving autos, and a miniature of his whole fantasy in one button.

Shackles of Justice

W · zone

Enemies caught in the zone around Sevaran are tethered and silenced while they remain inside it. Leaving the tether breaks it — but breaking it briefly stuns the one who fled. Stay and lose your spells, or run and eat the stun.

Counterplay built in: the decision is the point. Accept the silence, or accept the stun.

Intervention

E · targeted leap

A leap to an ally or enemy champion — usable only at medium range, never point-blank. On an enemy: slam down, deal damage and slow, with bonus damage if they're above 50% HP. On an ally: leap to them and shield both, with a larger shield if the ally is below 50%.

The minimum range matters: he pronounces judgment from a distance and crashes in. It keeps him from being too sticky once he's already on top of you.

Equilibrium

R · ultimate

Sevaran enforces balance across the fight: every ally is brought up to 50% health, and every enemy is brought down to 50% health. A great equalizer and a comeback button — and a catastrophe if you mistime it.

Design philosophy

Why he deserves to exist

Strengths

  • Teamfight disruption and anti-frontline pressure.
  • Manufactures comeback windows from behind.
  • Dangerous into tanks without raw true damage everywhere.

Weaknesses

  • Kiteable once his E is spent.
  • Cooldown-reliant; punished while abilities are down.
  • Vulnerable to poke and disengage; a mistimed ult is disastrous.

Counterplay

  • Leave the W tether and accept the stun on your terms.
  • Bait the ultimate, then fight after it's gone.
  • Engage when your team is already above 50% and theirs below.
  • Punish the minimum range on his Intervention.
About the work

I design champions the way Riot evaluates them: fantasy first, then clarity, counterplay, and the levers that keep them balanced.

These aren't pitches — they're a case study in design thinking. The goal of each concept is to show that I understand not just a cool fantasy, but readability, the decisions a kit forces, and the reasons a champion earns a place on the roster.
